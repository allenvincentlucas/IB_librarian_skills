# Gap 5: May 2027 Extended Essay Structural Changes

*Research notes for the Gap-Fill Addendum (Track 14) — added to the vault July 2026. In-text citations follow MLA 9th edition, with a bracketed number [n] alongside each for quick lookup against the numbered Works Cited list at the end.*

## 1. The two-pathway structure

Confirmed directly from the IB's own guide: interdisciplinary EEs require students to integrate the knowledge, concepts, theories, perspectives, or methods of two DP subjects, with the resulting essay assessed using the Subject-Specific Guidance for whichever two subject areas the student chose (Cartwright, "The Two New Pathways" [2]).

## 2. The five Interdisciplinary Frameworks — full list

Students choosing the interdisciplinary pathway must register their essay under exactly one of five frameworks, confirmed directly from the IB's guide:

1. **Culture, identity, expression**
2. **Digital society and design technology**
3. **Power, equality, justice**
4. **Sustainability, development, change**
5. **Economics and global politics**

Important nuance: a topic doesn't need to touch every word in its chosen framework — for "culture, identity, expression," a student could focus purely on identity, and the framework choice isn't directly assessed; it exists mainly to guide the student's inquiry and help the IB route the essay to appropriate examiners (International Baccalaureate Organization [7]).

## 3. The marking overhaul in detail

Beyond the 34→30 mark reduction: the previous standalone Criterion D (Presentation) has been phased out entirely, with its elements folded into the new Criterion A (Framework for the essay) — so presentation is no longer a separate judgment call, it's baked into how well the essay is framed. Critically, the same five criteria (A–E) now assess both pathways equally, with pathway-specific requirements built into how Criteria A and B are applied rather than having separate criteria per pathway (CASIE [4]).

## 4. What this means for the supervision workflow

Given the frameworks system, a useful pre-supervision move is having students "take their idea for a walk" — testing whether their topic could sit in more than one framework depending on which angle of the research question they emphasize, since the same topic (e.g., gender) could land under "power, equality, justice" or "culture, identity, expression" depending on emphasis. That ambiguity is by design, not something to resolve too early (Cartwright, "What's Different and What's the Same" [3]).

## Bottom line

The frameworks list itself (International Baccalaureate Organization [7]) is worth turning into a quick-reference handout for the first EE info session this year — students will need to pick one before registering, and having concrete examples per framework (which the IB guide itself provides) speeds up topic selection significantly.
