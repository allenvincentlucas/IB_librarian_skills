# Gap 4: Multilingualism & SSST Program Coordination

*Research notes for the Gap-Fill Addendum (Track 13) — added to the vault July 2026. In-text citations follow MLA 9th edition, with a bracketed number [n] alongside each for quick lookup against the numbered Works Cited list at the end.*

## 1. The librarian's role is explicitly named in IB's own materials

This is the strongest find here: the IB's own **SSST student guide** states directly that librarians may help SSST students compile a booklist, carry out research, and discuss literature — this isn't an inferred responsibility, it's written into the primary source document (UWC East Africa [8]).

Separately, Heeru Bhojwani's practitioner writing on IB Standards ties this to a specific accreditation practice: under Practice 1.1, the librarian's role is to learn and apply the school's Language Policy — providing multilingual resources, organizing storytelling sessions in multiple languages, and building a collection reflecting the community's cultural backgrounds (Bhojwani [1]).

## 2. How the program itself works

An SSST course lets a DP student study Language A: Literature in a language the school doesn't formally teach, with the school appointing an in-school supervisor (must be a DP Language A teacher) and the student also needing an external tutor for first-language support — the school's job is coordination and academic oversight, not instruction itself (Kuopio Lyseon Lukio [4]).

Course structure specifics worth knowing: recent updates (as of August 2024) reduced the number of required works from nine to seven over the two years, with at least four originally written in the study language and three in translation, all drawn from the IB's Prescribed Reading List (Surridge [7]).

Schools sometimes outsource the whole coordination burden:
- **Pamoja Education** now offers a fully-managed SSST Language A: Literature pathway (Pamoja Education [5]).
- A boutique **SSST Coordinator** service, built by a former DP Coordinator, specifically runs the logistics for schools that don't want to build this in-house (SSST Coordinator [6]).

## 3. Primary source documents

- Language A: literature SSST guide (English) — official reference (UWC East Africa [8]).
- IB's Prescribed Reading List — needed to select any student's works (International Baccalaureate Organization, "Prescribed Reading List" [3]).
- Working SSST LibGuide example — International School of Ulaanbaatar, credited to Teacher Librarian Eleanor Surridge (Surridge [7]).

## Video reference

- **Introductory video about SSST from the IBO** (hosted on Vimeo by the IB itself) (International Baccalaureate Organization, "Introductory Video" [2]) — overview of the course from a teacher's perspective and the challenges/expectations from a student's perspective: [Watch video ↗](https://player.vimeo.com/video/309488012)

*Usage note: this is an official IB-produced video — safe to show directly to staff or SSST students without any framing caveats.*

## Bottom line

Given the librarian is not currently the SSST supervisor, the highest-leverage move isn't becoming a language tutor — it's positioning the library as the **resource-and-booklist support role** the IB guide already names (UWC East Africa [8]), and making sure Language Policy documentation reflects multilingual storytelling and collection diversity per Practice 1.1 (Bhojwani [1]).
