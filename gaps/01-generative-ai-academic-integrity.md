# Gap 1: Generative AI Literacy & Academic Integrity

*Research notes for the Gap-Fill Addendum (Track 10) — added to the vault July 2026. In-text citations follow MLA 9th edition, with a bracketed number [n] alongside each for quick lookup against the numbered Works Cited list at the end.*

## 1. What the IB actually requires (the policy layer)

The core document is the IB's **Academic Integrity Policy**, updated with a dedicated appendix (page 53) titled *Guidance on the Use of Artificial Intelligence Tools*. Any AI-generated content included in assessed work must be credited in the body of the text and referenced in the bibliography, and students need to be taught to recognize the bias baked into AI output rather than accept it uncritically (International Baccalaureate Organization, *Academic Integrity Policy* [5]).

The IB has been explicit for years that it will **not ban** AI tools — it treats AI as inevitable infrastructure, comparing it to spell checkers, translation software, and calculators, and prefers to teach ethical use over prohibition (International Baccalaureate Organization, "Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools" [6]).

On citation mechanics — the IB's own FAQ is direct: the IB isn't claiming AI is a source in the traditional sense, but is seeking transparency (International Baccalaureate Organization, "Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools" [6]). Practically, that means the citation should include the tool used, the prompt given to it, and the date it generated the response — functionally the same shape as MLA's own generative-AI citation guidance (Modern Language Association [10]).

## 2. Where the librarian specifically fits in

A 2025 qualitative study of international-school librarians found that despite drawbacks, students already treat generative AI as a primary information-seeking source, and librarians consistently identify fact-checking as the essential skill to teach around it (Wong and Chiu [15]).

Zakir Hossain, IASL Vice President, developed an **AI Citizenship Framework** specifically built for teacher-librarians, with ready-to-use curriculum plans integrating AI literacy from an ethical, critical, and societal perspective (Hossain [4]).

A follow-up international survey of school librarians found the real bottleneck isn't willingness — it's capacity: limited time, lack of instructional materials, and minimal training are the critical barriers ("School Librarians' AI Literacy, Readiness and Professional Engagement" [13]).

## 3. What "good" looks like in practice

At **Suncoast Community High School** in Florida, a librarian became the AI-literacy lead for EE supervision specifically, building a public LibGuide covering brainstorming, research, writing, and citation with AI, used across a two-day EE workshop with juniors (Palm Beach County School District [12]).

At **John Street School**, librarian Paige Chambers uses AI tools herself — lesson planning, summarizing video content — before teaching them to students, a "model it yourself first" approach (*The 74* [14]).

## 4. Citation mechanics reference (school LibGuide examples)

International School of Kenya uses a quotation-mark plus prompt/date citation format, paired with a short academic honesty video (International School of Kenya [9]). International School of Beijing documents the EE authentication process alongside AI use (International School of Beijing [8]). Canadian International School Singapore offers a practical Dos/Don'ts list for EE and AI use (Canadian International School Singapore [1]). All three mirror the underlying format set out in MLA's own generative-AI citation guidance (Modern Language Association [10]).

## Bottom line

Three concrete deliverables would close this gap: (1) a one-page AI Statement of Use template for students, built off the IB's own citation guidance (International Baccalaureate Organization, "Artificial Intelligence (AI) Tools" [6]), (2) a staff PD session built around the Glanville audio clips and FAQ (Glanville [3]), and (3) adapting Hossain's AI Citizenship Framework rather than building instruction from scratch (Hossain [4]).
