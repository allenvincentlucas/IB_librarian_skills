<span class="eyebrow">MYP 1 → DP 2</span>

Scaffolding research skills across a 7-year secondary continuum requires moving students systematically from **guided consumption** to **independent, ethical production**.
In the IB framework, this map is anchored directly to the **Research** category of the Approaches to Learning (ATL) skills, which is broken down into *Information Literacy* and *Media Literacy*.
By structuring your library interventions around specific, developmental milestones, you ensure that students entering the Diploma Programme (DP) don't face a massive research "shock."
## The Scaffolding Matrix: MYP 1 to DP 2
| Programme Stage | Cognitive Focus | Library Intervention Target | Key Mastery Indicators |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Foundation**
*(MYP 1 - MYP 2)* | **Gathering & Verifying** | Keyword formulation, searching the catalog, identifying basic bias, introduction to a structured citation tool. | Can separate keywords from natural language; recognizes the difference between a blog and an encyclopedia; can build a basic bibliography entry. |
| **Intermediate**
*(MYP 3 - MYP 4)* | **Evaluating & Organizing** | Advanced Google search strings, navigating basic school databases, standard evaluation frameworks (like CRAAP), understanding unintentional plagiarism. | Uses boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT); filters search results by domain/date; correctly incorporates simple in-text citations and paraphrasing. |
| **Captsone Transition**
*(MYP 5 / Grade 10)* | **Formulating & Synthesizing** | Developing narrow research questions, initial academic literature searching, mapping data to arguments for the **Personal Project**. | Transitions from a broad topic to a focused, analytical question; synthesizes information from at least 3 distinct source types into a cohesive bibliography. |
| **Advanced Research**
*(DP 1)* | **Analyzing & Critiquing** | Navigating peer-reviewed networks (JSTOR/EBSCO), deep-dive citation formatting, identifying methodological limitations for **Internal Assessments (IAs)**. | Evaluates the authority of a peer-reviewed author; manages a multi-page works cited page; extracts data trends from academic studies. |
| **Independent Mastery**
*(DP 2)* | **Defending & Constructing** | Managing complex bibliographies, troubleshooting niche source citations, navigating copyright/ethical AI limits for the final **Extended Essay (EE)** submission. | Operates entirely independently in digital archives; self-corrects complex citations; applies academic integrity principles seamlessly to final publication-ready drafts. |
## 4 Actionable Strategies for Implementation
### 1. Unified Citation & Note-Taking Templates
The single biggest point of friction for secondary students is shifting formatting expectations.
 * **The Fix:** Work with your MYP/DP coordinators to standardize a single citation management tool (like NoodleTools, MyBib, or Zotero) and a unified note-taking method (like the Cornell System or digital research matrices) across all subjects school-wide. When students use the exact same research interface from MYP 1 through DP 2, cognitive load drops significantly.
### 2. The "Research Question" Diagnostic (MYP 5 to DP 1)
Students often try to write papers on topics that are far too broad (e.g., *"The history of the atomic bomb"*).
 * **The Fix:** Run a mandatory interactive workshop at the start of the MYP Personal Project and DP Extended Essay cycle using a **"funneling" template**. Teach them how to move a concept from a general Interest \rightarrow Topic \rightarrow Focused Question \rightarrow Working Argument.
### 3. Database Scaffolding
Do not open up college-level academic databases to an MYP 1 student; it leads to immediate frustration.
 * **The Fix:** Guardrails matter.
   * **MYP 1–3:** Teach curated, age-appropriate platforms (like Britannica School, World Book, or curated K-12 databases).
   * **MYP 4–5:** Introduce general academic search engines (like Google Scholar or basic Gale databases).
   * **DP 1–2:** Transition entirely to advanced academic repositories (JSTOR, EBSCO) and show them how to read abstracts first to judge a paper's utility before reading all 30 pages.
### 4. Co-Planned "Just-in-Time" Interventions
Isolated library orientation lessons at the start of the school year are usually forgotten by November.
 * **The Fix:** Integrate library interventions *only* when an assessment task demands it. Check the school's assessment calendar. If MYP 3 has a major Individuals and Societies (I&S) research paper due in Week 6, schedule your Advanced Search and CRAAP evaluation workshops for Week 2.
> **The Ultimate Goal:** By the time a student reaches DP 2, your role should shift from a *teacher of basic mechanics* (how to find a book or format a quote) to a *scholarly consultant* who helps them refine their arguments, locate highly niche source materials, and navigate complex intellectual property scenarios.
