# Operations, Automation, and Growth

## Starting a makerspace

Schools don't need a massive budget to start a makerspace. The most directly useful resource is **The Makerspace Librarian's Sourcebook** (ed. Ellyssa Kroski, Facet Publishing, 2017), which includes costed starter-kit tiers ranging from roughly $500 for a "Low Budget, Elementary School-Focused Makerspace Starter Kit" up to $50,000 for a "Dream Budget" milling/power-equipment setup [2]. A companion guide covers running a makerspace on a shoestring while tying activities to curriculum standards across language arts, math, science, engineering, and social studies [3]. Practical equipment-list guidance exists for when budget allows a 3D printer specifically [1] — a common failure mode is buying the printer before there's a training and space plan to use it.

**Video:** a walkthrough of a real school library makerspace, useful for pitching leadership on what a working space actually looks like [4]: [Watch video ↗](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=z-NmUv682qU)

Given no makerspace exists yet at most schools starting from scratch, the actionable next step is a $500-tier starter kit rather than jumping straight to 3D printing — the Kroski sourcebook's low-budget list is a ready-made shopping list [2].

## Automation for repetitive cataloging and admin tasks

Light Google Apps Script or Airtable workflows speed up repetitive catalog or reporting tasks. Google's own Apps Script documentation is the reference for scripting Destiny-adjacent or Google Workspace-based automations [5], and Airtable's template library gives working starting points rather than building from scratch [6].

**Video:** a beginner-friendly walkthrough of Apps Script for automating Google Sheets tasks [7]: [Watch video ↗](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8UmdqwY9AdA)

## The professional development timeline

Pacing PD across the school year, rather than trying to absorb everything at once, keeps the workload realistic:

- **Pre-Term (last week of July):** Read the IB *Ideal Libraries* guide and Standards & Practices notes on the librarian's role [8]. Pull current Destiny collection reports for a baseline. Check the CASIE [9] and IB PD workshop calendars [10] for anything librarian-designated in the Aug–Nov window. Skim the CREW manual's weeding decision matrix [11] so Term 1 starts from a documented framework.
- **Term 1 (Aug–Nov):** Collection health — CREW/MUSTIE weeding, DDC cataloging and Destiny administration, MoEHE/GCC compliance screening (full detail in [Module 5]({{ '/modules/05-collection-development-literacies/' | relative_url }})).
- **Term 2 (Nov–Feb):** IB-specific pedagogy — Extended Essay and Personal Project research supervision, ATL skills instruction, IB Standards & Practices fluency (full detail in [Module 1]({{ '/modules/01-ib-frameworks-pedagogy/' | relative_url }}) and [Module 2]({{ '/modules/02-research-extended-essay/' | relative_url }})).
- **Term 3 (Feb–May):** Data/BI skills for reporting (Power BI is a genuinely separate skill track worth its own study block rather than folding into general PD), automation workflows, and advocacy — see below.

## Data & BI skills for reporting

Circulation dashboards and collection-diversity reports double as accreditation evidence. If Power BI or a similar BI tool is a future goal rather than a current skill, it's worth scheduling as a discrete study block this term rather than treating it as incidental to librarianship — it's a genuinely separate track.

## Advocacy & committee reporting

A concise, evidence-based annual report template for the Learning Resources Committee draws on IASL's conference proceedings, which collect research and practice examples of advocacy framing [12], and the IB's *Ideal Libraries* document, which is explicitly designed as an advocacy tool for explaining the librarian's value to leadership [8].

## Professional communities worth joining

- **IASL** [12] — international, research-oriented, annual conference, small grants for research projects.
- **CASIE** [9] — authorized IB PD provider; check the calendar each term for librarian-designated workshops.
- **AISL** [13] — more US-independent-school-flavored but strong conference content.
- **GCC/Gulf regional librarian networks** — informal but often the fastest source for MoEHE-specific guidance; no stable public link, usually WhatsApp or Facebook groups tied to regional chapters.
